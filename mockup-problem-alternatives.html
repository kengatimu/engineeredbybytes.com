    The Problem

    We've heard the same story ten times.

    From bank CTOs, from fintech founders, from the engineers who hold it all together at 2am.

    The first thing they mention is the silence — a timeout.outage that customers tweet about before dashboards notice, a reversal.silent that finance only discovers three days later, a recon.manual that consumes the last weekend of every month in spreadsheets.

    Then come the operational scars: a audit.weeklong that costs an engineer five days every quarter, a deploy.midnight because daytime is too risky, and customer.complaints that take two days to answer — by which time the customer is already gone.

    The deeper costs surface last. Each new operator opens a security.surface nobody is staffed to monitor, while compliance.bolton retro-fits AML after every corridor goes live. New markets become a corridor.6mo integration project, and the cost.invisible is that eighty percent of engineering keeps payments alive instead of shipping product.

    If three of these feel uncomfortably familiar, we should talk.
