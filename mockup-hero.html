The Problem

Every payments shop has the same ten problems.

Yours included. These are the patterns we hear in every conversation with bank and fintech CTOs — across timeouts, reversals, audits, security, compliance, and the engineering cost of holding it all together.

  • timeout.outage Your customers see the outage before your dashboards do. Twitter is faster than monitoring.
  • reversal.silent A reversal fails silently. No alert fires. Finance discovers the gap three days later during reconciliation.
  • recon.manual Month-end reconciliation lives in spreadsheets. Four thousand rows. Manual matching. Weekend war rooms.
  • audit.weeklong "Show me Q3 reversals by reason code." One engineer. Five dashboards. Five days. Every quarter.
  • deploy.midnight Releases at 11pm because daytime is too risky. Rollbacks at 2am. On-call paged. Engineering trust erodes with every sprint.
  • corridor.6mo Every new operator is a six-month integration project. New protocol, new AML pipeline, new audit trail — built from scratch.
  • customer.complaints A missing transaction. Five systems to query. Two days to give the customer an answer — by then they're gone.
  • security.surface Every new integration is a new attack vector. Ten operators equals ten places encryption can be wrong — and ten audit findings every year.
  • compliance.bolton AML, KYC, and sanctions screening retro-fitted after every new corridor goes live. Compliance is always playing catch-up to engineering.
  • cost.invisible Eighty percent of your engineering team keeps payments alive. Twenty percent ships the products that actually win business.
