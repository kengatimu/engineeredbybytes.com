§2 Problem — six bespoke design concepts

Each concept uses the same eight pains so you can compare apples-to-apples. Scroll through, pick the one that doesn't feel like a B2B template you've seen before.

A

Terminal log stream

Production logs from your ops team's terminal. Each pain is a real-feeling incident with timestamp and severity tag. Speaks directly to engineering credibility — the buyer thinks "yes, this is exactly what my Datadog looks like at 2am."

payments-prod ~ tail -f /var/log/payments.log
11:47:23 CRITICAL customer.timeout 504 — partner unreachable, status UNKNOWN, dashboard alert in 7m
02:14:08 CRITICAL reversal.failed silently 3 days ago — found by finance during reconciliation
09:22:51 WARN audit.query in progress for 4d 6h — 5 dashboards, manual SQL
16:03:44 WARN corridor.activation pending — 176 days elapsed, 3 integration teams engaged
02:47:12 CRITICAL deploy.rollback initiated — production down 12m, on-call paged
04:31:19 WARN security.surface +1 — new operator integrated, attack vectors now 11
23:58:00 WARN reconciliation.manual — 4,217 rows exported to spreadsheet, weekend war room scheduled
10:00:00 INFO team.allocation — 80% maintenance, 20% product, sprint planning at 14:00

Why this works: instantly speaks the language of engineering buyers. Risk: non-technical execs (CFO, business heads) might find it alienating.

B

Audit findings report

Each pain is presented as a finding in a formal payment-infrastructure audit report. Numbered, severity-tagged, with status. Banker-credible aesthetic — looks like a CBK or internal-audit document the CTO would actually receive.

INTERNAL · CONFIDENTIAL

Last quarter: 4 rollbacks · All initiated after midnight
F-006 HIGH

F-008 CRITICAL
Engineering capacity consumed by maintenance, not product delivery

Why this works: looks like a real audit document — the CTO instantly recognises the format and severity language. Banker-credible. Risk: feels heavy / serious; less viscerally emotional than the terminal.

D

Sticky note war room

Each pain is a sticky note on a corkboard, slightly rotated, in different colours — like a real incident-response wall. Handwritten font, pushpins, the organic chaos of a payment ops war room. Playful but recognisable.

Why this works: visually distinctive — no fintech site looks like this. Captures the chaotic-but-human feel of real ops. Risk: could read as too playful for the most conservative banker. The handwritten font is the make-or-break choice.

E

Cost receipt — "What this costs you"

Each pain becomes a line item on a printed receipt with a quantified cost (engineering-hours, dollars, customer churn). Running total at the bottom. Translates abstract "pain" into concrete business cost — perfect when a CFO co-buyer is in the room.

COST OF DOING NOTHING
Mid-size bank · annual impact
RECEIPT #2026-Q3 · UNRESOLVED
Timeouts & outages 312 eng-hrs
Silent reversal recovery KES 4.8M lost
Regulator query response 160 eng-hrs / yr
Corridor build (per integration) 6 mo × 3 teams
Midnight rollbacks & pager 48 incidents / yr
PCI scope expansion +$120K audit
Weekend reconciliation 52 weekends
Maintenance-not-product 8 of 10 FTE
EST. ANNUAL ~$840K + opportunity
— THIS PRICING UPDATES ANNUALLY —

Why this works: single most powerful for finance / executive buyers — translates pain into a dollar figure. Memorable. Receipts are a universal metaphor. Risk: the numbers need to be defensible. CTOs will mentally validate them — if one feels wrong, all credibility goes.

F

Editorial / pull-quote

One large quote from a real-feeling CIO leads, with a clean numbered list of supporting pains below. Magazine-spread aesthetic — restrained, premium, banker-respectful. Closest in spirit to existing EBB design language.

In every payments shop we visit
"We deploy at midnight because we don't trust our own pipeline. Finance finds reversal gaps on Friday. Our team is in maintenance mode, not building anything new."
— CIO, East African tier-1 bank · paraphrased from two recent conversations
The eight problems we hear in every conversation
  • 01Customers spot outages before your dashboards do.
  • 02Reversals fail silently — found by finance days later.
  • 03Audit responses consume your team for a week.
  • 04New corridors take six months — every time.
  • 05Deployments break at midnight; rollbacks at 2am.
  • 06Attack surface grows with every new operator.
  • 07Reconciliation consumes weekends in spreadsheets.
  • 08Engineers in maintenance mode, not shipping products.

Why this works: matches EBB's existing editorial brand voice (serif headlines, restraint, premium). Quote framing makes it feel grounded in real conversation. Risk: least visually distinctive — closest to "normal" website territory.
