Hero animation — five options

Each design solves the same problem: visualise that EBB sits between the bank's corebanking and many operators, without the current cluttered six-stacked-labels look. Scroll through and pick.

01

Minimal flow — three nodes, no list

Three nodes: your bank, EBB, every operator. The "many operators" claim is made in words, not visualised individually. Single packet pulses through.

+ Cleanest. Eye focuses on flow direction.

− Loses specificity (no operator names visible).

02

Constellation — operators arc around EBB

EBB at the centre. Six operators arranged in a semicircular arc rather than stacked vertically. Pulses radiate outward, suggesting connection without dominating the right side.

+ All operators visible. More dynamic shape.

− Still six labels; some overlap risk on small screens.

03

Two-row — operators as chips below

Top row: Bank → EBB Gateway with breathing room. Bottom row: six operators as small horizontal chips. Removes the vertical wall; reads left-to-right.

+ Familiar reading direction. Less visual weight per operator.

− Loses the radial "from EBB" metaphor.

04

Rotating — one operator at a time

Bank → EBB → one operator. The operator label cycles through all six every 3 seconds. Always reads as "EBB connects to X" where X is changing.

+ Most dynamic. Each operator gets its own moment.

− Reader needs to wait to see all six. Movement might distract.

05

Abstract burst — no operator labels

Bank → EBB on the left. Right side: a fan of small unlabelled dots radiating from EBB — visual shorthand for "many operators." Specific operator names live in the body copy, not the animation.

+ Most architecturally elegant. Doesn't compete with the headline.

− No specifics visible. Risk: feels too abstract.
