Design 1 — four ways to make it feel alive

All four keep the same three-node architecture (Bank → EBB → Every operator). What changes is how much motion, how many concurrent elements, and how visibly "active" EBB feels.

Variant A

Continuous river — multiple packets always in flight

Same three nodes. But instead of one packet at a time, three packets traverse the line concurrently, staggered every second. The flow never stops; there's always something moving.

+ Continuous motion. Suggests real volume.

− Subtle — might still feel "calm" rather than "exciting."

Variant B

Burst — packet arrives and explodes into many

Single packet flows Bank → EBB → Every operator. When it arrives at "Every operator", five mini-dots burst outward in a small fan — visualizing the "many" without naming them.

+ Dramatic moment of arrival. Tells a story.

− Quieter between bursts. Risk of "wait for the show" feeling.

Variant C

EBB as engine — strong pulse from the centre

Three layered pulses radiate outward from EBB continuously, plus a packet flow. EBB visibly does work; it's the heartbeat of the system.

+ Most "alive" feeling. EBB feels powerful.

− Heavy on EBB; the "every operator" side gets less attention.

Variant D

Premium — energy hub + mini-burst + flowing line

Everything together: EBB pulses as the centre, the connection line itself flows (dashed crimson moving along), multiple packets traverse, and the "Every operator" node bursts into mini-dots. Maximum motion.

+ Most dynamic. Genuinely "exciting." Multiple layers of activity.

− Most movement = highest distraction risk. Might compete with the headline.
